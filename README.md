# ChipuRobo

**Nurturing Practical Skills Education in Africa**

ChipuRobo delivers inclusive, hands-on AI and robotics education across Kenya
using locally fabricated kits — bridging classrooms and emerging technology for
learners of every ability.

---

## Mission

> Deliver inclusive AI and robotics training with locally built equipment.

We weave digital fabrication into the CBC curriculum, launch Code Clubs, and
design accessible kits for every learner.

## Vision

> Build Kenya's leading inclusive tech education network.

Every student should access AI and robotics through locally fabricated,
inclusive tools — empowering **4 million youth across Africa by 2030**.

## What we do

One year-round programme, three stages. A learner meets us during outreach
through the Young Scientists Kenya (YSK) network, steps up to an intensive
Microsoft ADC bootcamp, then trains across the year through research projects
and Code Clubs, culminating in a national showcase at CEMASTEA. CBC aligned
throughout.

### Programmes

**AI Literacy Bootcamps** — Hands-on AI and robotics education aligned with the
CBC framework, delivered through locally fabricated kits. 5-day intensive,
15–20 students. Bootcamps run at the Microsoft Africa Development Centre in
Westlands, Nairobi, with participants receiving CEMASTEA-validated
certificates.

**Code Clubs (Raspberry Pi)** — Weekly after-school clubs co-run with the
Raspberry Pi Foundation for secondary schools, covering Raspberry Pi computing,
Python programming and hardware projects. Teacher training included.

**Inclusive Robotics** — Accessible technology including Braille challenges and
adaptive tools, built on universal design principles and multi-sensory
learning. 8–12 students.

### Also offered

- **PET Recycling Programme** — converting plastic waste into 3D printing
  material for robotics education
- **Teacher Training** — professional development in AI and robotics
  integration, delivered quarterly with CEMASTEA
- **Digital Fabrication** — 3D printing and maker skills using locally sourced
  materials, integrated into all programmes
- **ChipuRobo Internships** — 3-month post-programme placements helping
  students commercialise their projects

## Built inclusive by design

Inclusion is the starting point, not an add-on. We work with partner schools
serving visually impaired and other neurodiverse learners, with sustained
engagement rather than one-off visits.

Every programme ships with Braille worksheets, Kenyan Sign Language video, and
neurodiverse-friendly pacing. Student projects have themselves produced Braille
kits, KSL videos and neurodiverse-friendly AI lessons.

Schools in our maker-space network fabricate robots, Braille devices and
accessibility hardware for the rest of the network.

> Names of partner schools serving learners with disabilities are deliberately
> not published, to protect the learners and educators at those schools.

## Sustainability

> Innovation and sustainability go hand in hand.

Our PET recycling initiative transforms plastic waste into 3D printing
filament, creating the very robot parts our students learn with. This closes
the loop between environmental responsibility and technological education,
while creating economic opportunities for communities.

**The cycle:** collect plastic bottles from communities and process them into
filament → 3D print robot parts, enclosures and mechanical components →
students learn with robots built from recycled materials.

Our fabrication lab at the historic **Nairobi Railway Museum** is the hub for
these operations, where we also train youth and women in sustainable
manufacturing. The initiative has expanded beyond Kenya, with machines and kits
shipped to Nigeria through the ZeeTech Foundation.
